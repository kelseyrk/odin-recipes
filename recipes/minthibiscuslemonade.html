Mint Hibiscus Lemonade

Description

I'm growing Roselle hibiscus in my garden for the first time this summer. The calyx at the base of its flowers are dried and used for hibiscus tea. Combined with my mint planter, there will be a lot of hibiscus and mint combos happening in my kitchen later this year! (Store-bought would also be fine.) Now I just need to track down a lemon tree...

Ingredients

  • 0.5 cups dried hibiscus calyces
  • Handfull torn mint leaves (plus fresh leaves for garnish)
  • 8 cups water, divided
  • 1 cup fresh-squeezed lemon juice
  • 1.5 cups granulated sugar

Directions

  1. Mix hibiscus and mint leaves with 4 cups water in a large jar, let steep overnight. (You can also just drink this herbal tea mixture after this step!)
  2. Combine lemon juice, 4 cups water and sugar in saucepan, simmer on low heat until mixture is a syrup.
  3. Strain hibiscus and mint out of tea mixture, mix with lemon syrup. Chill.
  4. Serve over ice, garnish with fresh mint leaves if desired.
